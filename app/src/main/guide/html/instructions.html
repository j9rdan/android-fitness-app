How to use we-lift.

Signing up

Users will first be taken to the sign up page on their first time running the app. Otherwise, you are taken to the main home screen that displays your workout calendar.

we-lift. assumes all non-registered users will want to create a new gym plan, thus users are prompted to enter information required to do so.

Users will provide their:

  • Fitness goal (build muscle or strength)
  • Sex, age, height and weight
  • A muscle to target (optional)
  • Preffered split (no. of workouts per week)

Logging in / program creation

Once the above data has been submitted, a program will be generated, including the muscle a user may have chosen to target. The custom plan will also allocate initial weights for each exercise.

After programs are created, users will be taken to the home screen which displays the selected split, program type and upcoming workout. The program schedules workouts for the next X days after creation, where X is the user's split. For example, if a program is begun today on a 4-day split, we-lift. schedules workouts for the next 4 days, alternating between muscle groups.

Completing a workout

Pressing start or the current calendar date will begin today's workout, and once complete, users will fill in a difficulty rating for each exercise out of 10, with 10 representing working sets that required maximum effort, and 0 representing exercises that were too easy.

These figures are then used to calculate the new volume and adjust the weights for your next exercises. Pushing yourself in the gym is required to see any growth, but you cannot push until failure on EVERY SET. Hence, the goal is to find a challenging-enough weight that is still safe to use.

Some fitness jargon

A lot of words get thrown around when considering the topic of fitness. For clarity, here are a few common terms to help ensure anyone and everyone can use we-lift.

  • Split: distribution of a workout program over a given week
  • Hypertrophy: (simple definition) growth in muscle after a stimulus from working out
  • Volume: reps x sets x volume
  • PPL: common workout split (Push, Pull, Legs)
    • Push: targets chest, triceps and shoulders
    • Pull: targets back and biceps
    • Legs: targets all lower body
